# AI Research Highlights

## Scope

Notable developments in AI research, with a focus on technical substance.

- New research papers with novel techniques or significant results (arXiv, conferences)
- Open-source model releases with technical details (architecture, training, benchmarks)
- Benchmark results and methodology comparisons
- Infrastructure and tooling advances (training frameworks, inference optimization)
- Safety and alignment research with concrete findings

## Skip

- Product launch announcements without technical substance
- Funding, hiring, and corporate news
- Opinion pieces, hype content, and speculation
- Incremental updates without meaningful novelty

## Research Strategy

**Mandatory first step — crawl primary sources.** Before any web searches, use WebFetch on these URLs and extract recent posts/papers from the last 2 weeks:

1. `https://arxiv.org/list/cs.AI/recent` — recent AI papers
2. `https://arxiv.org/list/cs.CL/recent` — recent NLP/LLM papers
3. `https://www.anthropic.com/research` — Anthropic research
4. `https://openai.com/research` — OpenAI research
5. `https://deepmind.google/discover/blog/` — DeepMind research blog

For each page, identify posts/papers from the last 2 weeks not already in state, and WebFetch promising ones for details.

**Then do broader research:**
6. Search for notable model releases, benchmark results, and technical blog posts
7. Cross-reference claims with at least 2 sources
8. For papers, use WebFetch to read the abstract and introduction

**Verification rule:** Every claim must trace to a real, fetchable source URL. Do not write about anything you cannot verify with a primary source.

## Writing Style

**Target: 600-800 words per entry.**

Write like a knowledgeable colleague explaining something interesting they found.

- **Structure**: problem context, key insight, method, results, implications
- **Be concrete**: include specific numbers, comparisons, and benchmarks — not just qualitative claims
- **Explain the "so what"**: why does this matter beyond the paper itself?
- **End with open questions** or what to watch for next
