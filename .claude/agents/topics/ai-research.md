# AI Research Highlights

## Scope

Notable new research in machine learning and AI, with emphasis on depth over breadth.

Academic:
- Papers with novel methods or surprising results (NeurIPS, ICML, ICLR, ACL, arXiv preprints)
- New training techniques, architectures, and optimization methods
- Significant benchmark results that change the state of the art
- Reproducibility studies and negative results (often underreported but valuable)

Industry:
- Open-source model releases with technical details (architecture, training data, evaluation)
- New developer tools and frameworks with genuine technical innovation
- Infrastructure advances (training efficiency, inference optimization, deployment)

## Skip

- Product launches without technical substance
- Funding announcements, hiring news, executive moves
- Opinion pieces, hype content, "X will change everything" posts
- Beginner tutorials and listicles
- Benchmark results without methodology explanation

## Research Strategy

1. Search arXiv for recent preprints in cs.AI, cs.LG, cs.CL
2. Check major ML conferences for new accepted papers
3. Look for open-source releases on GitHub trending
4. For papers, use WebFetch to read the abstract and introduction for accurate detail
5. Cross-reference claims with at least 2 sources

## Writing Style

**Target: 800-1200 words per entry.** Write like a researcher presenting at a lab meeting — technically precise but conversational.

For academic papers, structure each entry around:
- **Problem**: What specific problem does this work address? Why is it hard?
- **Key insight**: The core idea that makes this work different. Explain the mechanism, not just the name.
- **Method**: How does it actually work? Enough technical detail that a technical reader can grasp the approach.
- **Results that matter**: "Closes 60% of the gap between X and Y" is more useful than "achieves 78.3% on benchmark Z."
- **Limitations**: What doesn't this solve? What are the caveats?

For industry/product entries:
- **What's technically new**: Not just "company released X" but what capability change it represents
- **Developer impact**: What was hard before that's now easy?
- **Ecosystem context**: Where does this fit? What does it compete with or complement?
