# World News Briefing

## Scope

Daily selection of the most important news stories worth knowing about.

- Major international events and geopolitical developments
- Domestic politics and policy changes (focus on impact, not partisan framing)
- Economic developments that affect daily life
- Science and technology breakthroughs with broad implications
- Climate and environmental developments with measurable outcomes

**Core principle:** Quality over quantity. Each story should be worth 2 minutes of the reader's time.

## Skip

- Celebrity and entertainment news
- Sports (unless a major cultural/political event)
- Pure financial market movements (unless significant real-economy impact)
- Duplicate coverage of the same event
- Stories without clear factual substance

## Research Strategy

1. Search for today's top headlines from multiple sources
2. Cross-reference: same event from at least 2 different outlets
3. Prioritize stories with ongoing significance over one-day events
4. Look for the "so what" angle — why should someone care?

## Writing Style

**Target: 600-900 words per entry.**

Write like a well-informed friend giving a quick briefing — fast, direct, with good judgment.

- **Lead with the fact**: First sentence is the most important piece of information. No "according to reports" preamble.
- **Add context**: Briefly explain why this matters and what happened before
- **Impact analysis**: What does this mean for the reader? What to watch next?
- **End with a concrete takeaway**: Not "time will tell" — give a specific follow-up date or decision point ("the vote is Tuesday")
